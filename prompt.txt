### STRICT NARRATIVE COMPLETENESS RULES (Most Important):

1.  **THE "STRANGER TEST" (MANDATORY):**
    -   Imagine showing this clip to someone who has NEVER seen the full video.
    -   Can they understand the story from START to FINISH without any prior context?
    -   If the segment mentions "he", "she", "that thing", "this guy" without introduction, it FAILS.
    -   If the segment starts in the middle of an idea and the viewer would be confused, it FAILS.
    -   **Every segment MUST be self-contained and independently comprehensible.**

2.  **COMPLETE NARRATIVE ARC (MANDATORY):**
    -   **BEGINNING (Hook - first 3-5s):** A strong opening that grabs attention AND sets up the context. The viewer must immediately understand WHAT the story is about and WHO is involved.
    -   **MIDDLE (Development):** The story develops with details, tension, humor, or value. This is where the audience stays engaged.
    -   **END (Conclusion/Punchline):** A satisfying conclusion. The story must feel COMPLETE. Never cut mid-sentence or mid-idea. The viewer should feel the story has ended naturally.

3.  **NO "GARBAGE" STARTS:**
    -   NEVER start with filler words ("Um", "Uh", "So...", "And then", "Aí", "Então né").
    -   NEVER start with low-energy intros ("Hi guys, welcome back", "Fala galera").
    -   NEVER start mid-conversation without context.
    -   **ALWAYS** start with a Hook: A strong statement, a question, a surprising fact, or an action.
    -   *Bad Start:* "(10s) So, I was thinking about that..."
    -   *Good Start:* "(12s) I almost died yesterday."

4.  **NO HANGING ENDINGS:**
    -   NEVER end mid-sentence or mid-thought.
    -   NEVER end with "and then..." or "so basically..." leaving the viewer hanging.
    -   ALWAYS end with a complete thought: a punchline, a lesson learned, a conclusion, a reaction.

5.  **CONTEXT INDEPENDENCE:**
    -   If someone says "That's why he said that" — WHO is "he"? WHAT did he say? If unclear, EXPAND the segment backward to include the introduction.
    -   If someone references a previous topic without re-introducing it, the segment FAILS.
    -   Better to make the segment slightly longer than to lose context.
